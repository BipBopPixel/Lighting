Chapter 01

What is Light?

Light is electromagnetic radiation detectable by our eyes — a tiny slice of a vast spectrum. Understanding this is the foundation of everything else in lighting.

← Ultraviolet 380 nm Visible Light 700 nm Infrared →
⚡

Speed of Light

Light travels at 299,792 km/s — the universal speed limit. It reaches Earth from the Sun in 8 minutes and circles the globe 7.5 times per second.

🌈

Wavelength = Color

450nm = blue, 550nm = green, 650nm = red. The human eye can distinguish roughly 10 million distinct colors across this narrow visible band.

👁️

Rods & Cones

Your retina has 120M rod cells for low-light vision, and 6M cone cells for color. Peak cone sensitivity is ~555nm — the green-yellow that LEDs are optimized around.

🌡️

Heat vs. Light

Incandescent bulbs convert ~90% of energy to heat, only 10% to light. LEDs flip this — over 90% becomes light. That efficiency revolution is what Signify was built on.

Chapter 02

The History of Lighting

From fire to photons — 400,000 years of humanity pushing back the dark. Every era changed how we live, work, and see the world.

~400,000 BC

Fire — The First Light

Controlled fire gave early humans warmth and the ability to see after dark. Cave paintings at Lascaux were made by firelight. Everything that followed is a refinement of the same idea: generate heat to produce light.

🔥
🕯️
~3,000 BC

Oil Lamps & Candles

Egyptians and Romans used tallow wicks for portable light. Beeswax candles were a luxury — cleaner burning and fragrant. For millennia, this was the ceiling of artificial lighting technology.

1792 – 1810s

Gas Lighting — Cities Wake Up

William Murdoch lit his home with coal gas in 1792. By the 1810s London's streets were gas-lit. For the first time, cities could function after dark — shops stayed open, crime fell, social life extended well beyond sunset.

🏮
💡
1879

Edison's Incandescent Bulb

Edison didn't invent electric light — he invented a practical, mass-producible version. His carbon filament lasted 1,200 hours and he built Manhattan's first power grid to go with it. Within decades, electric light replaced gas globally.

1938

Fluorescent — Efficiency Arrives

GE's first commercial fluorescent tube produced 3–4× more light per watt. Offices and factories adopted them fast. But they brought problems: mercury, flicker, hum, and color rendering that made everything look slightly wrong.

🔆
🔬
1962 → 1993

The LED Revolution

Holonyak's 1962 red LED was just an indicator for 30 years. Then Nakamura's 1993 bright blue LED — Nobel Prize Physics 2014 — unlocked white light: blue chip + yellow phosphor = white. Every LED in every Signify product today traces back to that discovery.

2012

Philips Hue — Smart Lighting Born

Signify launched the world's first mainstream connected lighting system: 16 million colors, app control, scheduling, and music sync. Lighting became software. A new product category was created — and you now work at the company that built it.

📱
🌐
2020s — Now

Light as a Platform

LEDs are standard. The frontier is AI, software, and biology: circadian-tuned light, daylight harvesting, UV-C air disinfection, LiFi data networks, and circular economy. Signify's mission: leading the way to a brighter world.

Chapter 03

Key Lighting Metrics

The vocabulary you'll use every day — when reading product specs, designing projects, or talking to colleagues and clients.

lm
Lumen
Total light output. More lumens = more light. The number on bulb packaging that replaces wattage as a brightness indicator.
60W equivalent LED → ~800 lm
lx
Lux
Illuminance — lumens per m². How much light actually reaches a surface. Varies by distance and angle from the source.
Office desk 300–500 lx · Surgery 10,000+ lx
cd
Candela
Luminous intensity in a specific direction. One candela is roughly the brightness of a single candle, hence the name.
Car headlight: ~15,000 cd
K
Color Temperature (CCT)
Measured in Kelvin. Low K = warm yellow-orange. High K = cool blue-white. Has nothing to do with actual temperature.
2700K home · 4000K office · 6500K daylight
CRI
Color Rendering Index
How accurately a source renders colors versus natural sunlight (CRI 100). Rated 0–100. Low CRI makes colors look flat and dull.
CRI 90+ for retail, hospitality, healthcare
lm/W
Efficacy
Lumens produced per watt consumed — the efficiency ratio. Higher = more light for less energy. The core LED advantage.
Incandescent ~15 · Good LED 100–200 lm/W
L70
Lifespan
Hours until a light source drops to 70% of initial output. The industry standard end-of-life definition for LED products.
Quality LED: 25,000–50,000 hours
UGR
Unified Glare Rating
Visual discomfort from glare. Lower is better. Critical for offices, schools, and any screen environment.
Offices require UGR <19 per EN 12464-1
R9
R9 Score
A CRI sub-score for saturated red rendering. Essential for skin tones, fresh food, and luxury retail. Ignored by basic CRI alone.
R9 >50 food retail · R9 >80 luxury fashion
°
Beam Angle
How wide light spreads from a fixture. Narrow beams create focused accent spots; wide beams give even ambient fill.
10–25° accent lighting · 60–120° general
SDCM
Color Consistency
MacAdam Ellipses: how visually identical two LED units appear. Matters when installing many fixtures side-by-side.
SDCM ≤3 required for retail & hospitality
Hz
Flicker
Light intensity variation per second. Invisible flicker still causes headaches and eye strain. Quality LED drivers eliminate it.
Flicker-free: DC drivers above 1,000 Hz

Chapter 04

Types of Light Sources

From Edison's filament to smart connected LEDs — how each technology works, its tradeoffs, and where it still fits today.

🔴

Incandescent

A tungsten filament is heated until it glows. The original electric light — beautiful warm color (CRI ~100) but 90% of energy is wasted as heat. Phased out by EU, US, and most markets due to efficiency regulations.

CRI ~100~15 lm/W~1,000 hr life2700K
💛

Halogen

An improved incandescent — halogen gas recycles tungsten back onto the filament, making it brighter and longer-lived. Still used in car headlights and professional studio photography where perfect CRI is non-negotiable.

CRI ~100~25 lm/W2–5k hoursVery hot
🔵

Fluorescent / CFL

Electric current excites mercury vapour to emit UV light. Phosphor coating converts UV to visible light. 4× more efficient than incandescent — but contains mercury, produces flicker, hum, and poor color rendering. CFLs bridged the gap before LEDs took over.

CRI 60–8560–100 lm/W8–15k hoursHas mercury
⭐

LED — Light Emitting Diode

A semiconductor emits light when current flows through a p-n junction. No filament, no mercury, minimal heat. The dominant technology today and the core of everything Signify makes. Tunable from 1800K to 6500K, dimmable, connectable, and extraordinary long-lasting.

CRI up to 98100–200 lm/W25–50k hoursNo mercury
🔮

OLED — Organic LED

Thin organic compounds emit light across an entire flat panel — not a single point. No glare, no shadows, no hot spots. Ultra-diffuse illumination you can see on phone and TV screens. Now emerging in architectural lighting as costs fall.

Zero glareThin & flexibleHigh costEmerging tech
📡

Smart / Connected Lighting

LED + wireless connectivity + software. Lights that respond to occupancy, daylight levels, time of day, or manual control via app or voice. Tunable white shifts CCT through the day to match circadian needs. Philips Hue is the world's best-selling smart ecosystem — made by your employer.

Tunable CCTApp + VoiceEnergy savingNeeds network

Chapter 05

How Light Affects Us

Light is the most powerful biological time-cue for the human body. It shapes mood, productivity, sleep, and health — often completely below our awareness.

Color Temperature Through the Day

1800K2700K4000K5500K6500K
🌙

Evening (2200K)

Warm amber mimics sunset. Reduces melatonin suppression and signals sleep preparation to your brain.

🌅

Morning (3000K)

Gentle warm light eases you awake. Standard for hotel breakfasts, cafés, and wake-up light therapy devices.

💼

Work Hours (4000K)

Neutral white maximises alertness and concentration. The standard across offices, schools, and healthcare facilities.

☀️

Peak Focus (5000K+)

Cool daylight-equivalent boosts serotonin and suppresses melatonin. Used in windowless labs and precision workspaces.

🧠

Circadian Rhythm

Your body clock responds to blue-rich light by suppressing melatonin. Misalignment — from phones, wrong office lighting, or shift work — causes chronic sleep disruption and health consequences.

📈

Productivity

Research shows 4000–5000K lighting increases alertness, reduces errors, and improves reaction time. Inadequate workplace lighting is a leading cause of fatigue — an issue Signify's Interact platform directly addresses.

🎭

Mood & Emotion

Dim warm light creates intimacy and relaxation. Bright cool light energises and sharpens focus. Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) is directly linked to insufficient winter light — treatable with high-lux bright light therapy.

🛒

Retail Psychology

Warm light (2700–3000K) with high CRI and R9 makes food look fresh and skin appear healthy. Light direction shapes texture perception. Lighting is retail's most powerful and frequently underestimated tool.

🏥

Healthcare

Human-centric lighting in hospitals accelerates patient recovery, reduces medication errors, and stabilises nurses' sleep on shift rotations. Light therapy is clinically proven for depression and circadian disorders.

🎓

Learning

Dynamic classroom lighting — brighter and cooler during tests, warmer during creative work — measurably improves focus, reading speed, and information retention in students across all ages.
